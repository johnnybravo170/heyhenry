Worker app /w — built for the phone, not the laptop.

The field surface for employees and subcontractors at HeyHenry. Today’s job, log time in two taps, snap a receipt, bill the GC. Open this on your phone — desktop is a fallback, not a design target.

Why phone-first

This is the field half of the product. Tradespeople use it outdoors, with gloves, on spotty signal. Every choice — ≥44px (lean bigger) targets, camera-first capture, pickers over keyboards, bottom-anchored primary actions, offline-tolerant writes — is anchored in that reality. A laptop view of the same product wouldn’t be wrong, it would be off-task.

01

4 tabs, not 8.

Today · Calendar · Projects · Profile, with a raised Log action. Tasks · Time history · Expenses · Invoices reached from Today or Log.

02

Capture-now, clean-later.

Time / expense / photo writes queue offline and sync with an explicit "saved, will sync" state. Nothing is silently dropped.

03

Same surface, capability-gated.

Employees and subs share one shell; worker_type is an owner setting that toggles Expenses and Invoices. The worker never sees the word "subcontractor."

04

✦ Henry accelerates capture.

Voice → time. Receipt OCR. Invoice-from-unbilled-work. Henry drafts · the worker confirms · never auto-submits money.

Hard boundaries

What the worker never sees.

  • No customer financials. No margin, no markup, no project total.
  • No other projects. Only the projects this worker is assigned to.
  • No dashboard or contacts. Middleware redirects to /w; RLS blocks the rest.
  • No "subcontractor" label. They see "Time," "Invoices" (if enabled), and a profile.
